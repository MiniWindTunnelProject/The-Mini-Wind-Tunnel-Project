About

The Mini Wind Tunnel Project is bringing exciting, university-level aerodynamics experiments to the school classroom.
Its open, modular design provides a low-cost platform for educational institutes, and has potential as a legitimate research tool





  • October 2019

    Project Beginnings

    The project started as a single low-cost demonstrator for outreach events, funded by the Engineering and Physical Sciences Research Council.

  • March 2020

    The Open-Hardware Vision

    Further funding was secured from The Univeristy of Bath's Alumni Fund to push development towards an open-hardware vision

  • August 2022

    Project Sponsorship

    ebm-papst sponsor the project - donating 10 EC fans to power the first fleet that will be donated to schools in need

  • July 2020

    First Public Showcase

    We were kindly invited by RS Components to showcase the latest design at their
    2022 REflect event

  • October 2022

    Open Hardware Makers Program

    We are accepted onto the Open Hardware Makers 2022 cohort. This 8 week program will guide us to better align with open hardware principles, and provide a foundation for complete poject documentation

    How do I contribute?

    We are always looking for contributors to help with design, documentation and educational material. Please don't hesistate to get in touch

  • The school giveaway program

    The school giveaway program donates mini wind tunnels free-of-charge to deserving schools in the UK. We are looking to address representation issues surrounding diversity, gender equality and income inequality in higher education. If you feel your school fits the bill, please get in touch
